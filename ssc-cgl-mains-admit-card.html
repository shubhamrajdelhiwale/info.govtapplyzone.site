SSC CGL Selection Process

The SSC CGL selection process consists of multiple stages designed to assess a candidate’s overall ability. Tier-1 is conducted as a screening stage and is qualifying in nature. Candidates who qualify Tier-1 are shortlisted for Tier-2 examination, which plays a crucial role in the preparation of the final merit list.

  • Tier-1: Computer Based Examination (Qualifying)
  • Tier-2: Computer Based Examination (Merit deciding)
  • Document Verification

SSC CGL Exam Pattern 2026

Tier-1 Examination

Tier-1 exam consists of objective type questions from General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude and English Comprehension. The exam is conducted for a total of 200 marks with a duration of 60 minutes. There is negative marking of 0.50 mark for each incorrect answer, therefore accuracy is equally important along with speed.

Tier-2 Examination

Tier-2 examination is the most important stage of SSC CGL as it decides the final merit. Paper-1 is compulsory for all candidates and includes sections on Mathematical Abilities, Reasoning & General Intelligence, English Language & Comprehension, General Awareness and Computer Knowledge. Additional papers are conducted for specific posts such as Statistics and Finance & Economics.

Tier-2 marks are used for final selection and post allocation. Performance in this stage is critical.

SSC CGL Syllabus 2026

The SSC CGL syllabus is designed to test both conceptual understanding and practical application. Quantitative Aptitude focuses on arithmetic, algebra, geometry and trigonometry. Reasoning includes verbal and non-verbal logic. English tests grammar, vocabulary and comprehension skills, while General Awareness covers history, geography, polity, economics, science and current affairs.

SSC CGL Salary Structure

SSC CGL posts are offered under different pay levels ranging from Level-4 to Level-8 as per the 7th Pay Commission. The salary varies depending on the post and department, but overall SSC CGL jobs provide attractive pay, allowances, job security and career growth opportunities.

SSC CGL 2025–26 Preparation Strategy

To crack SSC CGL, candidates should focus on concept clarity, regular practice and revision. Solving previous years’ question papers and mock tests helps in understanding the exam pattern and improving time management. Special attention should be given to Tier-2 preparation as it determines the final selection.
