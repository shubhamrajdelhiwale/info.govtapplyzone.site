What is SSC CGL Examination?

Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level Examination, commonly known as SSC CGL, is one of the most competitive and prestigious examinations conducted by the Government of India. This examination is organized every year to recruit graduate candidates for various Group-B and Group-C posts in Ministries, Departments, Constitutional Bodies, Statutory Bodies and Tribunals under the Central Government.

SSC CGL is considered a career-defining exam for lakhs of aspirants because it offers permanent government jobs, structured promotion hierarchy, timely salary increments, allowances as per the 7th Pay Commission and postings across India. The respect and stability associated with SSC CGL posts make it one of the top choices among graduate-level competitive examinations.

Every year, candidates from different educational backgrounds such as science, commerce, arts and professional streams appear for SSC CGL. The examination tests a candidate’s analytical ability, numerical aptitude, language proficiency, general awareness and computer knowledge.

SSC CGL 2025–26 Notification Details

The SSC CGL 2025–26 notification is the most important document for every aspirant. It contains complete details regarding the examination and recruitment process. The notification specifies the total number of vacancies, post-wise distribution, department-wise allocation, eligibility criteria, age limits, educational qualification requirements, age relaxation rules, reservation policy and selection methodology.

Candidates must read the notification carefully before filling the online application form. Any mismatch in eligibility criteria, incorrect information or violation of instructions can lead to cancellation of candidature at any stage of the recruitment process.

The notification also explains the rules regarding normalization of marks, tie-breaking criteria, medical standards for certain posts and the procedure for post preference filling.

SSC CGL Selection Process – Step by Step

The SSC CGL selection process is designed to ensure transparency and fairness. It evaluates candidates on multiple parameters including knowledge, speed, accuracy and overall aptitude.

Tier-1 examination acts as a screening test. Although it is qualifying in nature, candidates must score above the category-wise cut-off to be shortlisted for Tier-2. Marks obtained in Tier-1 are not added to the final merit list.

Tier-2 examination is the most crucial stage as it decides the final selection and post allocation. Performance in Tier-2 directly impacts the rank of the candidate. After Tier-2, shortlisted candidates are called for document verification.

  • Tier-1: Computer Based Test (Qualifying)
  • Tier-2: Computer Based Test (Merit Deciding)
  • Document Verification

SSC CGL Tier-1 Exam Pattern – Explained in Detail

SSC CGL Tier-1 examination consists of objective type multiple choice questions divided into four sections: General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude and English Comprehension.

The Tier-1 exam carries a total of 200 marks and is conducted for a duration of 60 minutes. Each question carries 2 marks. For every incorrect answer, 0.50 mark is deducted as negative marking. Due to negative marking, blind guessing should be avoided.

SSC CGL Tier-2 Exam Pattern – Complete Breakdown

Tier-2 examination is conducted in multiple papers depending on the post applied for. Paper-1 is compulsory for all candidates and includes multiple sections. Additional papers are conducted for specific posts such as Junior Statistical Officer and Assistant Audit Officer.

Tier-2 marks are used to prepare the final merit list. Candidates must focus heavily on Tier-2 preparation to secure a good rank.

Tier-2 is the final deciding stage. Even a small difference in marks can lead to a significant difference in rank and post allocation.

SSC CGL Syllabus 2026 – Subject Wise Detailed Explanation

The SSC CGL syllabus is vast and requires systematic preparation. Quantitative Aptitude includes arithmetic topics such as percentage, ratio and proportion, profit and loss, simple interest and compound interest, along with advanced topics like algebra, geometry, trigonometry and mensuration.

Reasoning syllabus includes both verbal and non-verbal reasoning. Topics such as analogy, classification, series, coding-decoding, syllogism, blood relation, direction sense and logical reasoning are frequently asked.

English Language section tests grammar, vocabulary, sentence structure, error spotting, comprehension skills and usage of words. Regular reading and practice are essential for scoring well.

General Awareness includes history, geography, polity, economics, science and current affairs. Static knowledge along with current events is required for this section.

SSC CGL Salary Structure & Career Growth

SSC CGL posts are offered under different pay levels ranging from Level-4 to Level-8 as per the 7th Pay Commission. Salary varies depending on the post, department and posting location.

Apart from basic pay, employees are entitled to Dearness Allowance, House Rent Allowance, Transport Allowance and other benefits. Career growth opportunities through promotions and departmental exams make SSC CGL jobs highly attractive.

SSC CGL Cut-Off – How It Works

SSC CGL cut-off marks vary every year depending on factors such as number of vacancies, difficulty level of the exam and number of candidates appearing. Separate cut-offs are released for Tier-1 and Tier-2.

Tier-1 cut-off is used for shortlisting candidates for Tier-2, while Tier-2 cut-off decides final selection.

  • Not revising regularly

SSC CGL Preparation Strategy – Long Term & Short Term

A well-planned preparation strategy is essential to crack SSC CGL. Candidates should start with understanding the syllabus and exam pattern, followed by concept building, regular practice and revision.

Mock tests play a crucial role in improving speed, accuracy and time management. Analysing mock test performance helps identify weak areas.
